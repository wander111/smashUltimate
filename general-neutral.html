Instances of a game

Since in a game there can be many instances in witch you need to play defensive or offensive, this translates to situations in witch I like to call "little games", this are interdependent instances.

The chase game

  1. Connect a d-tilt or up-tilt.
  2. Trow an aerial.
  3. If the aerial connect, do step 2.
  4. If air dodged, follow them and do step 1.

This is very simple on paper, but to success you need to have in mind the aerial options of your opponent, like the range of their moves or frame data.

The range game

Of course Cloud has range in their moves, but the true is that a lot of characters has more range in the same moves that Cloud has, and by range I also refer to how disjoint the move is.

Against some moves like Ness fair Cloud up-air is a bad option because most of the time it will cause a trade, so nair is a better option if Ness is above you.

Cloud has the privilege to have a projectile, the blade beam , so by definition it can out range everything, but of course it is not optimal in every situation, like trowing it in close quarters, but if the opponent is like half the stage afar, you can use it against moves like Bayonetta side b , since the option of trow a d-tilt will cause a trade, also you can use up b against it.

up-smash has a very important role in the range game because it can beat a lot of landing options, like Link dair , Corrin Dair , Cloud dair and Yoshi down-b .

So you get the idea, Cloud has an optimal move for every encounter, you just have to keep in mind the "my move vs your move" in the range variable and know if a Cloud move will:

  • Be beaten (out ranged)
  • Trade
  • Beat (out range)

The frame data game

Some of you are expert of frame data and some others don't like to keep in mind numbers or don't know how to interpret those numbers. The thing is there are instances who are crucial to know this, and one of the most common is in footsies situations. The frame data game is like in the range game "my move vs your move" but in this case the variable is not the range, but the speed, so for instance if you are playing footsies against Shulk, and you expect him to attack, if you trow a jab you will win 100% of the times, Shulk mains know this so they try to never be at a distance in witch jab 1 connect, this is because Shulk jab is frame 5 and Cloud jab is frame 4.

Another example is an out of jump situation against Marth. Cloudnair is frame 5 and Marth fair is frame 6 so although you lose the range game you will win the frame data game so nair is very useful if you raw it. If you want to win the frame data game, you need to know at least the frame data in Cloud moves.

How to encounter an approach

Sometime you will face an aggressive opponent who tries to approach you with an aerial attack or aerial special move. This is the instance in witch Cloud is very proficient because he has one of the best anti air game so the first topic in this area is:

The anti air game

Cross slash beat most of the aerial approaches in footsies and bair beat a lot of them in close quarters. up-air is useful to use when you predict a jump in footsies.

How your opponent plays

If your opponent is based on a reactive play stile, you, most of the times, can not make predictions of what he/she is going to do so you won't connect a move that is supposedly anticipating an action.

For example, if Cloud has limit and your opponent is in 150%, you and him know Cloud can kill with a single limit cross slash. You use back trow to trow them off stage and then you jump and use limit cross slash because you think he is gonna jump because he wants to evade the hit using jump. That read is not gonna happen to a reaction based player because he is seeing what you are going to do in order to make a decision, rather than make a decision based on a possible survival chance.

Type 1 player: make choices based on what he think has a hight rate of success, they want to anticipate the future (proactive thinking).

Type 2 player: make choices based on their opponent choices, they react to the past rather than anticipate the future (reactive thinking).

You are going to do more reads on a type 1 player because sometimes you know what is his "high rate of success" option, conversely its more difficult to make reads on a type 2 player because what they like is to wait, and do a reactive/counter action, unless you force them to a dice situation in witch they have not window to react but make a type 1 player choice or make a random choice.

Cloud needs to play depending on what type of player you are facing, that's why one of the first things you need to do is to recognize in witch category they fall. As a Cloud main, you also need to see witch type of play stile to execute, that's because being always proactive or always reactive will turn you, in the eyes of a good player, in someone predictable, although there are some super good players who stick to one play stile.

We generally don't make the choice of what type of player we are going to be, we never ask "'I'm going to be a reactive or a proactive player?" that thing come naturally to us, but It is important in a point of your "smash career" to recognize it and make changes in order to grow.

  • Reactive : Reacting to the past rather than anticipating the future
  • Proactive : Acting before a situation becomes a source of confrontation or crisis
